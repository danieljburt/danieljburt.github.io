Dan Burt

Postdoctoral Researcher · daniel.burt@mpimet.mpg.de

I am an ocean biogeochemist with a passion for research into the representation of carbon and nitrogen cycling processes at different spatial scales. I am currently a postdoctoral researcher in the Ocean Biogeochemistry Group of Prof. Dr Tatiana Ilyina at the Max Planck Institute for Meteorology in Hamburg, Germany after completing my Doctorate of Natural Sciences with the International Max Planck Research School for Earth System Modelling. My past research has focused on investigations into the carbon cycle and ocean-based Negative Emission Technologies using observations and models. In the future, my goal is to use a broad-range of methods, including observations, modelling and machine learning-based approaches to further research into carbon and nitrogen cycling and their roles in the Earth system.

I recently defended my doctoral thesis on Unravelling the climate conditions preceding the End-Permian Mass Extinction: Insights from latest Permian interannual tropical variability and extreme events as part of the German Research Foundation Research Group 2332: Temperature related stresses as a unifying principle in ancient extinctions. My current postdoctoral research position provides me the opportunity to complete the publication of research articles based on the findings described in my thesis as well as continue supporting the model development of the Hamburg Ocean Carbon Cycle model which is used by the research group of Prof. Dr Tatiana Ilyina across the Universität Hamburg, Helmholtz-Zentrum Hereon and Max Planck Institute for Meteorology.
